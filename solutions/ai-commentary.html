How our AI commentator works

Spalk's AI translates your live source commentary feed into any target language in real time. To ensure the translated voice carries the right emotional weight, the AI references a licensed archive of over a million hours of live sports commentary and sampling how a goal call, a last-second winner, or a tense moment actually sounds in that language, and matching that energy in the output. The translation doesn't just get the words right. It gets the feeling right.

Infrastructure for scalable AI-driven commentary

Every component needed to deliver translated, emotionally accurate commentary across unlimited languages and broadcasts inside your existing production environment.

AI-trained commentary engine

Trained on over a million hours of licensed sports commentary to understand how emotion, pace, and energy shift across specific match moments, so the AI knows a goal sounds different from a penalty, in every language.`

Generative voice synthesis

Uses generative AI to replicate tone, cadence, and emotional pacing of real commentators.

Multilingual voice replication

Reproduces the same voice style across multiple languages while preserving delivery structure.

Hybrid human and AI workflow

Human and AI commentary run side by side in the same platform so that you can use AI for volume coverage and human talent for marquee events, without switching tools or workflows.

Usage-based compensation system

When a commentator's voice model is used commercially, Spalk tracks usage and triggers payments automatically ensuring talent is compensated fairly every time their AI voice goes to air.

Consent-based voice governance

Every voice model on Spalk requires explicit opt-in from the commentator before it can be used commercially with full permission controls visible to both the talent and the rights holder.
